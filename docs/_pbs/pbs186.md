Before we switch our focus back to building statically generated websites with Jekyll, we need to take one more short diversion. Before we took a break from the topic we described the mechanisms for developing the *look* of a Jekyll website, and after our break we'll shift our focus to how Jekyll allows you to structure your content. In the modern world, that means ensuring the content you publish on your Jekyll sites produces effective link previews in social media apps. The OpenGraph protocol was designed to solve that problem. It originated within what was then Facebook, but it has matured into a widely adopted standard.
